GEA Certified Consultants

GEA Certified Consultants play a crucial role in implementing the Global Excellence Assembly (GEA) Standard within client organizations. They guide the strategic alignment, operational integration, and continuous improvement efforts to achieve sustainable excellence. Their key activities include conducting gap analyses, developing tailored solutions for organizational improvement, managing change, and facilitating the entire certification process. They ensure organizations understand and adhere to the GEA Standard, leading to certified operational excellence and sustainable business practices.

GEA Open Source Community

The GEA Open Source Community is a pioneering initiative dedicated to fostering shared knowledge and promoting excellence. It provides an open, collaborative platform where individuals and organizations can access, contribute to, and leverage a collective repository of expertise and best practices. This initiative aims to advance global standards of excellence, facilitating organizational success across various sectors.

GEA Certified Assessors

GEA Certified Assessors, operating under the Global Excellence Assembly Licensed Assessment Body (GLAB), are certified professionals who evaluate organizations against the GEA Standard. They conduct audits to ensure compliance with the standard's principles, aiming to improve and sustain organizational performance. Their responsibilities include analyzing processes, identifying improvement areas, and verifying the effective implementation of corrective actions to maintain their certification status.

GEA Licensed Assessment Body

A GEA Licensed Assessment Body (GLAB) is an organization authorized/ licensed to conduct formal assessments of organizations against the Global Excellence Assembly (GEA) Standard. They verify adherence to established criteria for operational excellence, aligning with global best practices. GLABs manage the certification process, ensuring organizations meet the GEA Standard through rigorous audits and assessments. Their role is technical and regulatory, encompassing audit planning, execution, reporting, and follow-up to guarantee continuous compliance and improvement within client organizations.
